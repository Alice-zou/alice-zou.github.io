Introduction

Synthetic Aperture Radar is a technique established in the 1950s. Carl Wiley, most viewed as the founder of SAR, started to focus on airborne radar. In 1951, he found that radar reflection from discrete objects in a passing radar beam field each had a minute Doppler, or speed, shift relative to the antenna. He could use antenna 1/100 the size of traditional radar to create detailed radar images. He applied for the patent for Simultaneous Buildup Doppler in 1954, which was considered the first SAR patent. Soon in 1960 the further study of SAR successfully won the competition to build imaging radar for the Lockheed SR-71 Blackbird --- the fastest jet in the world.

SAR Project Details

Surfside Condo Collapsed , The Leaning Tower of Pisa , San Francisco's sinking Millennium Tower. These big events above that happened around the world may all be caused by one thing: subsidence.

On June 24, 2021, around 1:20 am, half of the condo suddenly collapsed. The rescue went on for weeks, and finally, 95 deaths and 2 missings write a period for this event. Although the tragedy cannot be directly concluded from the satellite images, Miami Beach had suffered a 1-3 cm subsidence per year. The area where a building partially collapsed in Surfside, Florida, showed signs of land subsidence since the 1990s.

What can we do to avoid these tragedies? Fortunately, we already have a potential solution, called the Synthetic Aperture Radar.

What on Earth is Synthetic Aperture Radar? Synthetic Aperture Radar, or SAR, is a remote sensing tool that sends microwaves toward the Earth and collects the bounced back radar signal that describes physical features. It acts like a bat circling around the Earth and repeatedly sends microwaves toward the ground. Its “ears” collect bounced back waves and create a clear view of what happened on the ground. What differs the “Synthetic Aperture” radar from others is that it uses the spacecraft’s motion along with advanced signal-processing techniques to simulate a larger antenna. The device can rival the resolution of a 100 times larger antenna radar.

Millennium Tower in San Francisco encountered the same problem as Surfside’s condo. It has been confirmed over years that the building continued to sink for a few centimeters per year. Sentinel-1 satellites, when observing subsidence, use some more specific radar, called the InSAR. InSAR, or interferometric SAR, compares phrase differences between two complex radar SAR observations of the same area. InSAR focused more specifically on terrain deformation. The satellite sent C-band waves that have a frequency of 4-8 Gigahertz and a 7.5-3.8 cm wavelength toward the city. The waves successfully went through the skyscraper, passing obstacles to touch the ground. Then the waves bounced back toward space, losing some signal in the air, but some eventually returned to the satellite. After combining the phrase of two images with Differential Interferometry or DInSAR, it showed an up to 20 mm per year subsidence. This technique works well with buildings since they better reflex the radar beam.

The same technique is even applied to larger scale subsidence detection, exemplified by its employment in the whole Bay area. The bay area surroundings mainly have a green feature that senses no displacement or movement, but closely around the coastline, especially around San Rafael Bay, subsidence is clearly visible in red and orange. Being the high-tech area of the United States, it is hard to count the economic loss if a large collapse happened again in the Bay Area.

Another place with the similar problem as the Bay Area is Shenzhen, which had been observed subsiding since the 1980s. Both the Bay Area and Shenzhen are located around the coast. Geological displacement and subsidence are more extreme around the coastline. The purple dots in the graph indicate an up to 25 mm subsidence per year.

If Shenzhen and the Bay Area collapse due to subsidence again, the economic and scientific loss will be uncountable.

As Synthetic aperture radar techniques evolve, they can create a huge benefit for human society, especially in the detection of geological movements like subsidence, displacement, or ascension. For all of these city subsidences, we can all find out that the SAR can play the main role as a warning inside. The NiSAR satellite that is planned to launch in 2021 will return a tremendous amount of data. Indubitably, the widespread use of SAR will provide better protection for people in the future.

Summary

The Synthetic Aperture Radar has incredibly extensive usage. It has the ability to go through the clouds and have a peek at the Earth’s geological appearance. Some sensors such as P-band can even detect soil moisture. It acted as a scanning tool of the Earth, penetrating most obstacles. The comparison of subsidence for two high-tech areas of the world provides a structural example of SAR’s capability. Future city planning and geological movement warnings can all be based on the data of SAR.
